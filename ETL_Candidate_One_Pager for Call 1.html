CALL PREP GUIDE
You've been identified as an ETL with Store Director potential in the next 12–18 months.
This guide will help you walk into each breakout room with clarity, confidence, and real answers.
Group Talent
Development
💡
What this is: A series of three developmental calls throughout the year. Each call includes three breakout rooms — Personal Leadership, Guest Experience, and Operations. Facilitators will use guided questions to understand your scope, self-awareness, and readiness. Come prepared with real examples, honest gaps, and specific plans — not talking points.
Breakout Room 01
Personal
Leadership
Facilitated by Matt & Sean
Topic A
Know Thyself
How well do you know the leader you are — and the leader you need to become? Be ready to talk about your real strengths, your real blind spots, and how you've changed based on feedback.
How would your team describe your leadership style vs. how you'd describe it?
What's the hardest feedback you've received — and what changed because of it?
How does your leadership shift when you're under pressure?
Topic B
SD Job Description & Profile
Have you actually read the SD job profile — and honestly assessed yourself against it? Know what excites you, where you have gaps, and what your plan is to close them.
What part of the SD role are you furthest from — and what's your plan?
If given a store tomorrow, what would your first 30 days look like?
How are you currently developing the leaders around you?
Breakout Room 02
Guest
Experience
Guest Experience Facilitators
Topic A
Commitment vs. Compliance
There's a difference between a team that follows the service standard and a team that believes in it. Know which culture you're running — and be ready to explain how you got there.
What does committed service behavior look like vs. compliant service behavior on your floor?
When scores are fine but something still feels off — how do you respond?
How do you personally model guest obsession so your team actually sees it?
Topic B
SD Staffing & Scheduling
Scheduling is a strategic tool, not an admin task. Know how you use traffic data, sales trends, and team availability to build coverage that serves both guests and your team.
What data or signals drive your staffing decisions — how far ahead do you plan?
Tell me about a time a staffing gap hurt guest experience. What changed?
How should an SD's leader schedule differ from a functional area schedule?
Breakout Room 03
Operations
Operations Facilitators
Topic A
Elevating Efficiency
The best operators build systems that run without them. Be ready to talk about routines you've built, how you diagnose waste, and where you've improved efficiency without sacrificing team or guest experience.
What routines in your area run consistently — even when you're not there?
What operational problem is still unsolved — and why?
Topic B
My Target Dashboard
Know your numbers cold — and be able to tell the story behind them. Facilitators want to know if you read data as a signal or just a score.
What metrics do you check daily — and what story are they telling right now?
Which metric are you least confident interpreting — and what's your plan?
Topic C
The SD Lens
Can you read the full store — not just your area? SDs see people, product, and presentation simultaneously. Practice walking in and noticing everything.
What are the first three things you look for when you walk in — and what do they tell you?
Tell me about a time you saw a problem outside your area and acted on it anyway.
🎯
Be Specific
Bring real examples with real outcomes. Vague answers signal surface-level thinking. Know your stories cold.
🪞
Be Honest
Name your real gaps. Leaders who can't identify their development areas aren't ready for greater scope.
📈
Show Your Plan
For every gap you name, know what you're actively doing about it. Awareness without action isn't readiness.
🏪
Think Total Store
SD-ready leaders own outcomes beyond their department. Show that your thinking has already made the shift.
5 THINGS TO PREPARE BEFORE YOUR CALL
Do this work ahead of time — not in the room
01
Read the SD Job Profile
Pull it up. Score yourself honestly on each expectation. Know where you land before someone asks.
02
Know Your Dashboard
Review your current metrics. Be ready to explain trends, not just recite numbers.
03
Prep 3 Leadership Stories
One showing growth from feedback. One showing accountability. One showing cross-functional ownership.
04
Identify Your #1 Gap
Every strong candidate can name it. What's yours — and what are you doing about it right now?
05
Articulate Your "Why"
Why do you want to be a Store Director? Make it real, specific, and about impact — not the title.
