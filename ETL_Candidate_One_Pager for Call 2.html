Breakout Room 01
Personal
Leadership
Facilitated by Matt & Sean
Topic A
Be a Good Boss While Asking for More
The best leaders care deeply about their people and hold a high standard simultaneously. Be ready to show how you hold both — not one at the expense of the other.
How do you show genuine care for your team while consistently expecting more from them?
Tell me about a difficult performance conversation — how did you hold both the standard and the relationship?
What does a bad version of you look like as a boss — and how do you catch it?
Topic B
Distributing Competence
SDs can't be the most capable person in every corner of the store. Know who you've made more capable in the last 90 days — and where capability is still stuck in you.
Name a team member you've made meaningfully more capable. What did you do — and how do you know it worked?
If pulled from your area for a week, what would struggle — and what does that tell you?
Where are you still the go-to person for something your team should own?
Breakout Room 02
Guest
Experience
Guest Experience Facilitators
Topic A
Call to Action — GREET
Target's in-store brand promise is delivered one greeting at a time. Know how your team greets, helps, engages, and thanks guests — especially when you're not watching.
How do you build consistent greeting habits across your team — even when you're not on the floor?
What's the difference between a genuine greeting and a scripted one — and how do you develop that in your team?
Target has acknowledged inconsistent in-store experience. How are you making sure your team isn't part of that problem?
Topic B
Recognition vs. Accountability
Recognition and accountability are not opposites — they are a system. Know your ratio, know your team, and be ready to show that you use both deliberately and with precision.
How do you balance recognition and accountability in a typical week — what does that actually look like?
How do you make recognition feel genuine and specific — not routine or hollow?
How do you hold the standard without making accountability feel punitive?
Breakout Room 03
Operations
Operations Facilitators
Topic A
Leading at the Right Level
SD-ready leaders enable performance — they don't replace it. Know where you are operating below your level, what you're doing about it, and what the SD altitude looks like from where you stand.
How do you decide what to own vs. what to hand off — and how has that changed?
What work are you still doing that an SD would say belongs to your team?
When the floor is falling apart, what's the right leadership response at your level?
Topic B
Leadership Routines
Great SDs manage through rhythms, not reactions. Know your weekly leadership calendar, your pre-shift habits, your 1:1 cadence, and why each routine exists.
Walk me through a typical leadership week — what recurring touchpoints do you own and why?
How do you use a walk-through or LOD shift to do leadership work — not just operational checks?
Which routine is most effective and which isn't sticking yet — what's the difference?
THE CALL 2 MINDSET SHIFT
From knowing → to doing → to building
🔄
Show Evidence, Not Intention
Don't tell us what you plan to do. Tell us what you've already changed. Facilitators are looking for behavior, not aspiration.
📡
Lead Through Others
By Call 2, you should be building a more capable team around you. Who is doing more because of what you've invested in them?
🗓️
Routines, Not Reactions
Know your leadership calendar. SDs manage at scale through structured rhythms — not by putting out fires all day.
5 THINGS TO PREPARE BEFORE CALL 2
Come in with receipts — not plans
01
Name 1 Person You've Developed
Who is more capable today because of something you deliberately did? Know the story and the outcome cold.
02
Map Your Leadership Routines
Write out your weekly calendar. What are your recurring touchpoints? Which ones actually move the needle?
03
Know Your Recognition Ratio
In a typical week, how many times do you recognize versus hold accountable? What does that tell you about your culture?
04
Audit Your Altitude
Where are you still working below your level? Be honest. What specifically are you doing that belongs to your team?
05
Reflect on What Changed
Since Call 1, what is actually different about how you lead? Be specific. If nothing has changed, that is the most important thing to address.
