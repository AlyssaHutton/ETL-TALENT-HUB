Call 3 — Final Assessment
🎯
This is the year-end readiness determination. By the end of this call, your facilitators will answer one question: Is this leader ready to own a store in the next 12 months? Come prepared with your business story, Q4 evidence, and a clear picture of your store's consistency — including where it still falls short. Candidates who can name their gaps with confidence and a plan will be taken more seriously than those who can only name their strengths.
Breakout Room 01
Personal
Leadership
Facilitated by Matt & Sean
Topic A
Business Confidence & Business Acumen
Can you give a 2-minute business overview — results, trends, risks, and opportunities — right now, without notes? SDs lead business conversations, not just operational ones. Know your numbers. Tell the story.
Give me a 2-minute overview of your business right now — results, trends, risks, and opportunities.
What is one area where results are not where you want them — what is your theory, and what is your plan?
What is the biggest business risk you are managing right now — and what are you doing about it?
Topic B
Visit Kick-Off
If your DTL walked in right now, could you lead the first 60 seconds with confidence? Know your opening, know what you want them to see, and know how to own the gaps before they find them.
DTL just walked in. What do you say in the first 60 seconds?
What is one thing you would proactively share with a visiting leader before they had to ask?
How does your team perform on an unannounced visit vs. an announced one — and what does that tell you?
Breakout Room 02
Guest
Experience
Guest Experience Facilitators
Topic A
Peak Season Leadership
Q4 is the final exam for your guest culture. What standards do you hold no matter what? How do you keep your team energized and your guests feeling the brand promise at its busiest and most intense?
What changes about your guest experience leadership at peak volume — and what stays exactly the same?
Tell me about a Q4 moment where your leadership made a real difference for your team or guests.
How do you stay energized as a leader through a 6–8 week holiday grind — and how does that affect your team?
Topic B
Q4 Staffing, Scheduling & Training
Q4 execution starts in late summer — not October. Know your staffing plan, your seasonal training approach, your scheduling strategy for peak windows, and what you will do differently this year than last.
How far ahead do you plan Q4 staffing — and what inputs drive your headcount decisions by week?
What is your plan for getting seasonal team members from hired to guest-ready as fast as possible?
After Q4, how do you debrief — and how does that change how you plan the following year?
Breakout Room 03
Operations
Operations Facilitators
Topic A
Consistency in Fundamentals
Name your three strongest fundamentals and why they hold. Name the one that does not — and be specific. Facilitators are looking for a leader who has built execution into their culture, not just their presence.
Name three fundamentals you are most confident are executed consistently every day — and what makes them that way?
Name the fundamental you are least confident in — and tell me exactly why and what you are doing about it.
What happens to your fundamentals when you have high call-outs, reduced payroll, or back-to-back disruptions?
Topic B
Predictability vs. Chaos
Does your store feel the same every day — or does it depend on who is working, what time it is, and whether you are there? Predictability is built by design. Know what you have built and where entropy still wins.
What do you do deliberately to reduce variability in how your area operates shift to shift?
What is the biggest source of chaos in your current operation — and what is your role in it?
If a new SD walked into your area for the first time, what would tell them this is a well-led operation — and what might give them pause?
WHAT FACILITATORS ARE DECIDING AFTER THIS CALL
Three possible outcomes — know which one you are working toward
SD READY
Ready in the next 12 months
Consistent evidence across all three calls. Business fluency without prompting. Q4 proof points. Predictable, self-sustaining operation. Can name gaps and has active plans.
DEVELOPING
Strong trajectory — specific gap remains
Clear growth from Call 1 to Call 3. One or two meaningful gaps still present. On track with targeted development in the next 6–12 months before placement.
NOT YET
Continued development needed
Limited evidence of behavioral change across the year. Awareness has not translated into action. Business fluency, fundamentals, or culture gaps are significant. Re-evaluate next cycle.
5 THINGS TO PREPARE BEFORE THE FINAL CALL
This is the year-end close — bring your best evidence
01
Know Your Business Story Cold
Practice a 2-minute business overview out loud. Results, trends, one risk, one opportunity. No notes. Be ready to deliver it on demand.
02
Prep Your Visit Kick-Off
Write and rehearse your 60-second DTL opening. Know what you want them to see, and know what gap you will own before they find it.
03
Reflect on Q4
What worked in your Q4 staffing, scheduling, and training? What fell short? What will you do differently? Have specific examples ready.
04
Audit Your Fundamentals
Name your three strongest and your one weakest. Be specific about why each holds or does not hold — and what you are doing about the weak one.
05
Identify What Changed Since Call 1
The most powerful thing you can do in this call is show growth across the year. Be ready to name exactly what is different — in you, your team, and your operation.
